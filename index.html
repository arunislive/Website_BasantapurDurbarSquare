Welcome to Basantapur/Kathmandu Durbar Square:
Size and Location:
Basantapur Durbar Square, occupying an area of approximately 54,000 square meters (581,251 square feet), is centrally located in Kathmandu, Nepal. It lies at an altitude of around 1,400 meters (4,600 feet) above sea level, nestled within the Kathmandu Valley, which is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and stunning landscapes.
Construction and Builders:
Basantapur Durbar Square was built by the Malla kings, who ruled over the Kathmandu Valley from the 12th to the 18th century. The square and its surrounding structures were constructed over several centuries, with each successive king contributing to its expansion and embellishment. The intricate craftsmanship and architectural splendor of Basantapur Durbar Square are a testament to the skill and ingenuity of the Newari artisans who worked tirelessly to bring the royal vision to life.
Duration of Construction:
The construction of Basantapur Durbar Square spanned several hundred years, beginning in the early Malla period and continuing through successive dynasties. While exact records of the construction timeline are scarce, historical accounts suggest that the square and its key structures were gradually developed and expanded over the course of approximately 300 to 400 years. This prolonged construction period allowed for the incorporation of diverse architectural styles and artistic influences, resulting in the breathtaking ensemble of palaces, temples, and courtyards that define Basantapur Durbar Square today.
As you explore Basantapur Durbar Square, you'll be transported back in time to an era of regal grandeur and artistic brilliance. From the towering pagodas to the intricately carved wooden windows, every corner of the square bears witness to Nepal's rich cultural heritage and architectural legacy. Whether you're a history enthusiast, a cultural aficionado, or simply a curious traveler, Basantapur Durbar Square offers a captivating glimpse into the timeless beauty and enduring spirit of Kathmandu. Welcome to a world where history comes alive amidst the majestic splendor of Basantapur Durbar Square.
